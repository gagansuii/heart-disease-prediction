About the Heart Disease Prediction Tool

Project Overview

This heart disease prediction tool uses machine learning to estimate your 10-year risk of developing coronary heart disease (CHD) based on various health parameters. The model was trained on the Framingham Heart Study dataset, which tracked individuals over time to identify risk factors for cardiovascular disease.

By entering your health information, you can get an estimate of your heart disease risk and identify potential areas for lifestyle improvement. The tool also provides educational resources about heart disease risk factors and prevention strategies.

The Dataset

The dataset used for this project contains information about various risk factors and whether the individual developed heart disease within a 10-year period. It includes data from over 3,000 participants.

Key Features:
  • Demographic information:
    • Age
    • Sex
    • Education level
  • Behavioral factors:
    • Smoking status
    • Cigarettes per day
  • Medical history:
    • Blood pressure medication
    • Previous stroke
    • Hypertension
    • Diabetes
  • Physical measurements:
    • Total cholesterol
    • Systolic and diastolic blood pressure
    • BMI
    • Heart rate
    • Glucose levels
The dataset is based on the Framingham Heart Study, one of the longest-running cardiovascular cohort studies that has contributed significantly to our understanding of heart disease risk factors.
The Model

We use a Random Forest Classifier, which is an ensemble learning method that operates by constructing multiple decision trees during training. This approach helps to avoid overfitting and provides robust predictions.

Model Pipeline:
Data Preprocessing

The raw data is cleaned and prepared for model training:

  • Missing value imputation using median/mode
  • Feature scaling to normalize numerical values
  • One-hot encoding of categorical variables
Model Training

The Random Forest model is trained with the following characteristics:

  • 100 decision trees in the ensemble
  • Trained on 80% of the dataset
  • Optimized for balanced accuracy
Model Evaluation

The model is evaluated on a held-out test set (20% of data):

  • ROC-AUC score to measure discrimination ability
  • Precision and recall metrics
  • Confusion matrix analysis
Prediction

When you enter your health information:

  • Data is processed through the same preprocessing pipeline
  • Model predicts probability of heart disease
  • Risk factors are identified and highlighted
