Understanding Heart Disease Risk Factors

Heart disease remains one of the leading causes of death worldwide. Understanding the risk factors and their relationships can help in early detection and prevention.

The visualizations below provide insights into how different factors contribute to heart disease risk based on our dataset of over 3,000 individuals.

Data-Driven Insights

Explore the relationships between various health metrics and heart disease risk

Demographic Factors

Age Distribution by Heart Disease Risk
Key Findings:
  • Heart disease risk increases significantly with age
  • The risk begins to rise notably after age 50
  • Peak risk occurs in the 60-70 age range
Age vs. Cholesterol by Heart Disease Risk
Key Findings:
  • Both age and cholesterol levels contribute to heart disease risk
  • High cholesterol (>240 mg/dL) is a significant risk factor at any age
  • Older individuals with high cholesterol have the highest risk

Clinical Measurements

Blood Pressure and Heart Disease
Key Findings:
  • Higher blood pressure correlates with increased heart disease risk
  • Systolic BP above 140 mmHg is a significant risk factor
  • Diastolic BP above 90 mmHg is also associated with higher risk
  • Many individuals with heart disease have hypertension
BMI Distribution by Heart Disease Risk
Key Findings:
  • Higher BMI is associated with increased heart disease risk
  • BMI over 30 (obese) shows significantly higher risk
  • Even overweight individuals (BMI 25-30) show elevated risk

Lifestyle & Medical History

Smoking Status and Heart Disease
Key Findings:
  • Smokers have a significantly higher risk of heart disease
  • The percentage of heart disease cases is notably higher among smokers
  • Smoking is one of the most significant modifiable risk factors
Diabetes and Heart Disease
Key Findings:
  • Diabetes significantly increases heart disease risk
  • Individuals with diabetes have approximately twice the risk
  • Diabetes and heart disease share many common risk factors

Model Insights

Feature Importance
Key Findings:
  • Age is the most important predictor of heart disease risk
  • Blood pressure measurements are highly significant
  • Cholesterol levels play a major role in risk assessment
  • Smoking-related features are important predictors
Correlation Heatmap
Key Findings:
  • Strong correlation between systolic and diastolic blood pressure
  • Age correlates with several risk factors
  • Smoking status and cigarettes per day are highly correlated
  • Several risk factors show moderate correlation with heart disease

Model Performance

ROC Curve

The Receiver Operating Characteristic (ROC) curve shows the trade-off between sensitivity (true positive rate) and specificity (1 - false positive rate). The area under the curve (AUC) measures the model's ability to distinguish between classes.

Precision-Recall Curve

The Precision-Recall curve shows the trade-off between precision (positive predictive value) and recall (sensitivity). This is particularly useful for imbalanced datasets where the positive class (heart disease) is less common.

Key Takeaways

Major Risk Factors
  • Age: Risk increases significantly with age, especially after 50
  • Blood Pressure: Hypertension is a major risk factor
  • Smoking: Significantly increases heart disease risk
  • Cholesterol: High levels contribute to heart disease
  • Diabetes: Approximately doubles the risk of heart disease
Preventive Measures
  • Quit Smoking: One of the most effective ways to reduce risk
  • Control Blood Pressure: Through medication and lifestyle changes
  • Manage Cholesterol: Through diet, exercise, and medication if needed
  • Maintain Healthy Weight: Aim for a BMI between 18.5 and 24.9
  • Regular Screening: Especially important for those with multiple risk factors
